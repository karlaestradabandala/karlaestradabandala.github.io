My name is Karla Estrada Bandala, currently a 4th year at the University of California, Riverside. Iam pursuing a bachelors with a distinction in philosophy. My main philosophical interests include ethics and political philosophy. I am interested in pursuing a PhD in philosophy, and eventually becoming a Professor of Philosophy. Im interested in taking an interdisciplinary lense into my reserach, as it allows me to get a broader picture of the issues that I am reseraching. I recently finished some reserach on Terror Management Theory as part of the McNair summer reserach program. Under the guidance of John M. Fischer, I looked at the theory, outlining the problem of relativism, and posing a possible solution to the potentially dangerous outcomes of the theory. I did so by taking an interdisciplinary lense, as Ernest Becker did, who was the founder of the theory. This reserach on death has allows me to start thining about questions on personhood, specifically how we all come together and build relationships with others. The idea of community and relationships is very complex, and I believe that we cannot start with an isolated instance of the self, rather, we must take into account the web of relatinoships that we are born into, which dictates how we view not only ourselves, but the world. Outside of the academic world, I am the president and coach of Dancesport at UCR, a competitive ballroom and latin dancing club on campus. As president, I am responsible for making sure that our members are introduced into the ballroom world, as we participate in competitions. As instructors, I strive to make my latin classes accessible to all levels, making sure that people are constantly engaged in the art of dancing. We have two other student instructors that teach ballroom dances, such as walts and tango. I currently teach the lead part, and I teach weekly lessons on different dances, which include: samba, cha-cha, rumba, jive, paso doble. I was introduced to dancing in this club, and I pride myself on being able to extend and bring others into a different, diverse world, being able to express ourselves through dancing.
